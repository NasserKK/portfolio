UX/UI Case — Pirâmide 360 ERP

Migration from desktop ERP to Web + creation of the Gizé Design System.

CASE IMAGE HERE (hero)

Overview

Pirâmide 360 ERP is an enterprise platform used by mid and large-sized companies. The modernization required migrating more than 1300 screens to the Web, along with the creation of a scalable new Design System.

Problem Context

The original desktop system had technical limitations, compromised usability, and accumulated inconsistencies over the years — requiring a full rebuild and the creation of a Design System to standardize the entire platform.

My Role

1. Analysis & Understanding

Interviews with internal users, study of the system manual, and flow mapping.

2. UX Redesign

Reorganization of information hierarchy, flow simplification, and prototyping.

3. UI & Design System

Active participation in the creation of Gizé and new components.

4. Validation

Internal testing of critical flows and alignment with developers.

DESIGN SYSTEM COMPONENTS HERE

Redesigned Flow Example — Product Management

Before (Desktop)

  • More than 40 fields visible simultaneously.
  • Lack of visual hierarchy.
  • Fragmented process.
  • High error rate.

After (Web)

  • Divided into logical tabs.
  • New "field-group" component in the Design System.
  • Continuous flow on a single page.
  • Informative tooltips for fiscal fields.
